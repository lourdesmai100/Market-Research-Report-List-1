<p><p>The glucose excipient market comprises two primary types: anhydrous glucose and glucose monohydrate. Anhydrous glucose is a pure, water-free form used in pharmaceuticals and food products for its stability and sweetness. In contrast, glucose monohydrate includes one molecule of water, enhancing its solubility and making it suitable for specific applications such as in formulations requiring moisture retention. Both types serve crucial roles in diverse industries, catering to various functional needs in drug formulation and food processing.</p></p>
