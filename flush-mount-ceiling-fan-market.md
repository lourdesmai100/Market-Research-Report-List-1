<p><p>The flush mount ceiling fan market includes two primary types: AC ceiling fans and DC ceiling fans. AC ceiling fans operate on alternating current, typically offering robust airflow and are often more affordable. In contrast, DC ceiling fans use direct current, providing energy efficiency, quieter operation, and advanced speed settings. Both types are designed for low-ceiling environments, ensuring optimal airflow without sacrificing headroom. The choice between them often depends on energy preferences, budget, and desired performance features.</p></p>
